---
title: "Research in the Age of AI"
date: 2025-03-03
tags: ["research"]
---

Doing research in Japan in 2026 feels like standing at a crossroads. On one hand, you have the traditional, rigorous academic structure. On the other, you have the "AI explosion" that is fundamentally changing how we sit at our desks.

In foundational research (like the Math I struggle with), AI can be confidently wrong. It can invent citations or skip logical steps that are crucial for a peer-reviewed paper.

There is a thin line between using AI to explain a concept and letting AI do the thinking for you. If I let the machine summarize everything, I realize I’m not actually "absorbing" the knowledge.

Researching in 2026 means the bar for "originality" has shifted. It’s no longer enough to just process data; the value now lies in the intuition and the experimental design that an AI can't replicate (yet).
